Booking myths, adjudicated

Everything below is graded against fare-tracking data (mostly Google Flights' multi-year analyses) rather than folklore. Two of these myths are actually true — those are where the real money is.

METHODOLOGY — Booking windows and ◆ markers are anchored to published fare-tracking data, chiefly Google Flights' multi-year fare-path analyses (domestic low point ≈39 days out, low range 23–51 days; international lows ≈49+ days and region-dependent), plus their holiday-specific figures. The curve lines are schematics of market mechanics — demand mix, carrier mix, seasonality — not measured fares; no public dataset publishes true booking curves. Built by a friend who does this for a living; opinions are mine alone and not my employer's. Prices are never guaranteed — when you see a fare below the recent average inside your window, buy it.
