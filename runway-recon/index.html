How it works. Pick an airline and get a random airport from its full scheduled 2026 network, framed in a fixed 6 × 6 mile box, north up, no labels. You can zoom in for taxiway-level detail and pan around, but never out past the box. Each wrong guess reports miles off and points an arrow toward the mystery field — unless hard mode is on, which ditches the arrow. The approach lights fill in as you close the distance: all five green means you’re on the numbers. Guesses autocomplete from the chosen airline’s network.
